8  Offboarding

When a team member leaves the Brain Development & Education Lab, we want to ensure a smooth transition that protects lab resources, preserves institutional knowledge, and maintains positive relationships. The following sections outline the off-boarding process for departing team members and their supervisors.

8.1 Leave notification

  • Provide at least 2 weeks notice to your team lead (Carrie Townley-Flores or Adam Richie-Halford) and Jason Yeatman

  • Submit formal resignation to Jason Yeatman and Human Resources

  • Work with your team lead and Jason Yeatman to establish your last day of work

8.2 Offboarding timeline

8.2.1 2-4 weeks before last day

General knowledge transfer and documentation:

  • Document ongoing projects and responsibilities

    • Create detailed status reports for all active projects

    • Document any pending tasks, deadlines, or commitments

    • Update project management documentation (Monday.com, GitHub issues, etc.)

    • List all partnerships/collaborators and their current status

  • Organize and archive files

    • Ensure all work is saved to appropriate shared Google Drives (not personal drives)

    • Organize files with clear naming conventions and folder structures

    • Delete any duplicate or unnecessary files

    • Document location of important files and resources

  • Transfer specialized knowledge

    • Schedule meetings with team members taking over your responsibilities

    • Create how-to guides or documentation for specialized tasks

    • Share passwords for any shared accounts with appropriate team members (through secure methods)

    • Document any informal processes

    • Transfer ownership of any Google Drives

  • Update access documentation

    • Create a list of all systems, accounts, and access you currently have

    • Share this list with your team lead

Partnership-specific:

  • Transfer active partnerships to other team members

    • Create partnership handoff documents including partner contact info, history, preferences, and upcoming commitments

    • Introduce new ROAR contact to partners via email

    • Schedule transition calls if needed

  • Update Monday.com

    • Ensure all partnership records are current

    • Document any pending DUAs or IRB updates

    • Transfer ownership of relevant boards/items

  • Documentation updates

    • Update Partnerships 101 documents if you’ve learned new best practices

    • Document any partner-specific processes or preferences

    • Update outreach and recruitment templates if needed

Developer-specific:

  • GitHub:

    • Ensure all code is committed and pushed to appropriate repositories

    • Complete any pending pull requests or document their status

    • Document any work-in-progress features or bugs

    • Update README files and technical documentation

    • Transfer ownership of any repositories you created

    • Complete any pending code reviews

  • Development environment:

    • Document any unique development environment setup

    • Share any local configurations that might be needed

    • Document any dependencies or tools specific to your work

  • ROAR dashboard:

    • Document current status of any dashboard features in development

    • Transfer any open issues or tickets

    • Update ROAR Technical Manual if needed

    • Document any known bugs or technical debt

Researcher-specific:

  • Data and analysis:

    • Ensure all data is properly stored in appropriate Google Drive locations

    • Document data cleaning procedures and decisions

    • Transfer analysis code with clear documentation

    • Update data dictionaries if needed

    • Document research methodology and analysis plans in detailed READMEs

  • Publications and presentations:

    • Complete or transfer any pending publications

    • Document status of manuscripts in progress

    • Transfer presentation materials

    • Update PI on any conference commitments

  • Data security:

    • Confirm all participant data is removed from personal devices

    • Delete any local copies of sensitive data

    • Ensure compliance with data security and sharing policies

    • Return or securely delete any external assessment data

8.2.2 1 week before last day

Account Access Review:

Work with your team lead to review and plan for removal of access to:

  • Communication platforms

    • Slack (note: you will be removed from workspace)

    • Stanford email (will be deactivated)

    • ROAR partnerships email (if applicable)

  • Data and storage

    • Google Drive (specific folders)

    • BigQuery

    • Any other databases

  • Development and project management

    • GitHub (repositories and teams)

    • Monday.com

    • Canva

  • ROAR systems

    • ROAD super admin access

    • Any other ROAR-specific systems

  • Stanford systems

    • Building access

    • Axess

    • Other university systems

8.2.3 Last day

  • Meet with team lead for final check-in

  • Participate in exit interview (if scheduled)

  • Receive information about:

    • Final paycheck and benefits

    • Stanford email forwarding (if applicable)

    • References and recommendations

    • Alumni network opportunities

Equipment return

  • Return all Stanford and lab property to your team lead or designated person:

    • Laptop

    • Monitor

    • Keyboard

    • Laptop stand

    • Mouse or other peripherals

    • Stanford ID card (physical)

    • Building access cards/keys

    • Any other lab equipment or testing materials

Final access removal confirmation

  • Your team lead will coordinate with appropriate personnel to remove access to:

    • Slack workspace

    • Google Drive folders

    • GitHub repositories

    • Monday.com

    • BigQuery

    • ROAD super admin

    • IRB protocols (if listed as study personnel)

    • Building access

    • Stanford email

Final documentation

  • Submit any final timesheets or administrative paperwork

  • Complete benefits off-boarding with Stanford HR

  • Update personal contact information for future communication

8.3 Team lead offboarding checklist

8.3.1 Immediately upon notice

  • Notify Jason Yeatman and relevant stakeholders

  • Create off-boarding timeline

  • Assign interim coverage for critical responsibilities

  • Schedule knowledge transfer meetings

8.3.2 2-4 weeks before departure of team member

  • Review all active projects and partnerships

  • Identify permanent reassignments

  • Ensure documentation is being completed

  • Plan for any gaps in coverage

  • Review access list with departing team member

8.3.3 1 week before departure of team member

  • Confirm all knowledge transfer is complete

  • Verify all files are properly organized and accessible

  • Confirm equipment return logistics

  • Prepare access removal requests

8.3.4 Last day for departing team member

  • Collect all equipment and lab property
  • Submit access removal requests to:
    • Slack admin
    • Google Workspace admin
    • GitHub admin
    • Monday.com admin
    • BigQuery admin
    • ROAD admin
    • IRB admin (if applicable)
    • GSE IT for building access
    • Stanford IT for university systems
  • Archive employee files appropriately
  • Update team organizational charts and contact lists

8.3.5 Within 1 week after team member departure

  • Verify all access has been removed

  • Update lab website and directories

  • Redistribute responsibilities as planned

  • Follow up on any outstanding issues
