3  Onboarding

Welcome to the Brain Development & Education Lab! We’re excited to have you join our team. The following sections outline what to expect before and during your first few weeks.

We broke out the onboarding into general information and team-specific information.

3.1 General onboarding

3.1.1 Day 0:

Complete Stanford onboarding requirements

Approximately one week before your start date, you will receive a welcome email from Stanford’s Welcome Center with instructions to complete the following steps:

  1. Create your SUNet ID and password and enable Two-Step Authentication

    • You will need your University Employee ID number (provided in the email)

    • Follow the instructions in your welcome email to set up your sunet id. NOTE: Your SUNet is required to set up your work computer, so please complete this without delay

    • Test that your SUNet ID works by logging into the Axess portal

    • Reply to the Welcome Center email to confirm your SUNet ID is functional

  2. Download the Stanford Mobile application to get your virtual ID card

    • The app includes mobile Stanford identification (for building access), campus map, phone directory, and more

  3. Upload your photo for your Stanford Staff ID card

  4. Complete the GSE New Employee Form (Qualtrics link will be provided in the email)

    • Create a short bio with background information to share with GSE staff

    • Photo submission is optional

  5. Plan your commute

    • Stanford offers free transit passes for Caltrain, VTA, AC Transit, and SamTrans

    • If you need your pass for your first day, place an expedited request using the link in your Welcome Center email

    • For parking permits, complete the Parking Registration Form for temporary access, then purchase through the Online Ordering Portal once you’re in the system

  6. Send your photo to the project/product manager for the lab websites

    • Send a head shot photo and a small blurb about yourself and your interests to the project/product manager for the lab websites

I-9 verification

You will receive an email from the GSE Administrative Services Manager with instructions for I-9 verification, which must take place within 3 business days of your start date:

  1. Complete page 1 of the I-9 form (attached to email) before your first day and return via email

  2. Schedule a meeting to verify your ID documents (see attached list for accepted documents in the email)

Lab setup confirmation

The team-lead for your respective team (Jason Yeatman, Carrie Townley-Flores, Adam Richie-Halford) will confirm the following before your first day:

  • Start time and location

  • Deskspace assignment (if in-person)

  • Equipment setup: monitor, laptop stand, keyboard, laptop

  • Staff onboarding training schedule

3.1.2 Day 1 (Monday):

  • 9:00AM:

    • Meet team lead on Zoom (if first day is Monday or Friday) or in person on Stanford’s campus (if first day is Tuesday-Thursday).

  • 10:30AM-12:00PM:

    • Staff Onboarding Meeting on Zoom which provides context about Stanford’s culture, specific next steps, and introduces you to other new hires.

  • Afternoon:

    • CITI Training Group 7: IRB BioMed/GCP Research (All Medical Investigators and Staff)

    • Begin online Axess trainings

    • Review Brain Development and Education Lab Manual

3.1.3 Day 2 (Tuesday):

  • 9:30AM-10:30AM:

    • Meet the team

    • Lab space tour (if in-person)

    • Campus tour with your team and other lab members (if in-person)

    • Pick up physical Stanford ID card from the Tressidder Student Union (if in-person)

  • 10:30AM-11:00AM:

    • Continue online Axess trainings

    • Continue Lab Manual review

  • 11:00AM-12:00PM:

    • Benefits Training Webinar on Zoom

    • Important: You must enroll in benefits within 31 days of your start date

  • 12:00PM:

    • Lunch with the team (lunch is provided if in-person)

  • Afternoon:

    • Complete benefits paperwork

    • Continue reviewing Lab Manual

    • Begin reviewing team documentation (provided by team members or team lead)

    • Meet with relevant team members based on provided onboarding schedule to discuss general or team-specific onboarding topics (see onboarding topics below)

3.1.4 Day 3 (Wednesday):

  • Morning:

    • Check in with team lead, Carrie Townley-Flores or Adam Richie-Halford

    • Continue reviewing relevant team documentation/onboarding topics

  • 12:00PM-1:00PM:

    • ROAR All-Hands Meeting (lunch provided if in-person)

      • Please inform the research/partnerships coordinators of any food allergies/preferences.

      • All members are expected to help with cleanup following the conclusion of the meeting.

  • Afternoon:

    • Continue finalizing onboarding materials and review of team documentation/onboarding topics

    • Meet with team members based on onboarding schedule

3.1.5 Day 4 (Thursday):

  • Morning:

    • Check in with team lead, Carrie Townley-Flores or Adam Richie-Halford

  • 12:00PM-1:00PM:

    • Lab meeting (lunch provided if in-person)

  • Afternoon:

    • Meet with team members based on onboarding schedule

3.1.6 Day 5 (Friday):

  • All-day:

    • Final day of first week

    • Meet with team members based on onboarding schedule

    • Wrap up any outstanding onboarding tasks and review remaining onboarding topics

3.1.7 Important deadlines

  • Benefits enrollment: 31 days from start date

  • I-9 verification: 3 business days from start date

  • CITI training: Must be completed before working with data
