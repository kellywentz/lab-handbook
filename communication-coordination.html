4  Communication and Coordination

One benefit of the academic environment is its schedule flexibility. This means that lab members may choose to work in the early morning, evening, or weekends. That said, we do not expect lab members to respond outside of business hours (9:00AM-5:00PM PST).

4.1 In-person meetings

The majority of the lab works in-person Tuesday-Thursdays. On those days, it is common to meet in-person rather than online. If you plan to meet in person, please remember to book a meeting room.

In addition to one-on-one meetings and personal meetings, the lab hosts the following recurring meetings:

  • ROAR All-Hands on Wednesday 12:00-1:00PM PST (if remote, please check meeting invites or slack to find the zoom link).

  • Lab Meeting on Thursday 12:00-1:00PM PST (if remote, please check meeting invites or slack to find the zoom link).

Note

For the above meetings, lunch is provided. Please reach out to the research/partnership coordinators if you have any food allergies/preferences so they can correctly adjust the lunch orders. Please also notify the coordinators, if you plan to miss either meeting at the beginning of the week so they can adjust the order count to reduce food waste.

All members of the lab are expected to help with clean up following each of these meetings!

4.2 Slack

Slack is used as the primary form of internal communication.

  • Use Slack to contact all members of the lab.

  • Keep research project-related questions, topics, and updates in respective lab channels to keep the entire team in the loop. This helps to reduce shadow tasks and keeps necessary team members on the same page.

  • Please try to respond to internal messages with team members within 24 hours.

  • If you are unusually busy (e.g., grant writing, publication/conference preparation, midterms, etc.) please notify the respective teams and set your status on slack so the lab has an expectation of slow responses.

  • If you are on vacation or out sick please notify the team leads (Jason Yeatman and/or Carrie Townley-Flores/Adam Richie-Halford) and set your status on slack so people can reduce mentions in direct messages and channels.

  • If a thread is too long or if you have important information for the entire channel to see, please make sure to click “Send to channel”.

  • Slack has many helpful integrations with Outlook calendar and other extensions to help automatically set slack statuses based on meetings and other time blocks.

Important

If the channel is public, DO NOT share data via slack. Private channels and private DMs are encrypted and are safe to share data. When possible, share Google Drive links to dataframes rather than CSVs and other data files.

4.3 Email

Outlook email is used as the primary form of external communication.

  • Use Stanford email for messages to external ROAR partners (e.g., school representatives) and lab participants.

  • Generally, strive to respond to ROAR partners within 3-5 business days. As noted above, if you are unusually busy or on vacation please alert the team leads and team and write an Out of Office email with an alternative partnerships contact.

4.4 Online meetings

4.4.1 Booking on Outlook

Outlook calendar is used as the primary form of online and in-person meeting booking and tracking.

  • Lab members tend to check availability on Slack and then book a meeting based on response.

  • If you plan to be late, please inform the respective meeting attendees on Slack.

  • If you cannot attend, please notify in the meeting invitation RSVP and on Slack.

4.4.2 Hosting on Zoom

Zoom is used as the primary form of hosting online meetings. Zoom is very user friendly, provides AI integration for note-taking, easily records important meetings, offers easy-to-use screen sharing and other interactive features, and can host a large number of users (internal or external).

4.4.3 Hosting on Slack Huddle

Slack Huddle is used as the primary form of quick internal meetings. Often if lab members need to quickly chat for an off-schedule meeting, they will likely ask to meet on Huddle. It acts as a quick “phone call” that does not require a separate meeting ID or link. However, it does not offer as many helpful meeting features as Zoom.

4.5 Monday.com

Monday.com is used by the ROAR Partnerships team as their primary partnership coordination and management system. Monday.com is used in the following ways:

  • Organize partnerships by name, grade, and location.

  • Keep track of current and past ROAR administrations.

  • Document bi-weekly sprint tasks and accomplishments.

  • Map large research projects and the samples needed for each.

  • Track DUA processes for each incoming partner.

  • Track IRB updates and progress.

4.6 GitHub

GitHub is used by ROAR researchers and developers as their primary research collaboration and issues management system. GitHub is used in the following ways:

  • Collaboration on research projects.

  • Backing up and saving code.

  • ROAR Dashboard issue management/ticketing system.

  • ROAR developers task management system.

  • ROAR Technical Manual host.

  • ROAR Data Dashboard host.

  • roarutility R package host.

  • ROAR Dashboard host.

4.7 Google Drive

Google Drive is used as the sole form of data storage and shared documents for researchers, staff, and students. We store the following on Stanford’s secure Google Drives:

  • Meeting notes (internal and external)

  • School, district, student data

  • In-lab participant data

  • Presentations and feedback notes

  • Project management documentation

  • Testing materials and documentation

  • Validation data

Data access is provided in line with the principle of least privilege. If you need access to a specific folder or document, please contact Carrie Townley-Flores or Jason Yeatman.
